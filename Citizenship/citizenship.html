The N-400 is the name of the application for naturalization, and it requires many specific documents to go along with it. There is also a $725 application fee. Click here to learn about fee waivers.

Depending on your situation you will most likely need to attach additional documents, but everyone filing an N-400 needs:

1. A photocopy of both sides of your Permanent Resident Card

2. A check for the application fee.

3. Two identical color photographs of you with your name and Alien Registration Number.

Click here for a checklist of additional documents.

A visa is a conditional authorization granted by a country to a foreigner, allowing them to enter, remain within, or to leave that country. There are many different kinds of Visas, and it is important to know which you should apply for:

1. Immediate Relative and Family Sponsored

2. Employer Sponsored

3. Other Immigrants

A permanent residency card, or green card, grants the freedom to live and work in the United States freely. You may become eligible through your family, employment, status as a refugee, and more. Green Cards are renewable, allow the application for a work permit, and require a $1,140 application fee. Even with a green card, one may be subject to deportation if charged with certain crimes, fraud, or failure to report a change of address.

1. Be eligible for one of the immigrant categories established in the Immigration and Nationality Act (INA)

2. Have a qualifying immigrant petition filed and approved for you (with a few exceptions)

3. Have an immigrant visa immediately available and be admissible to the United States
