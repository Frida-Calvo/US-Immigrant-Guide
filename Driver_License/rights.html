"I am undocumented am I eligible to recieve a drivers license?"

Being undocumented does not limit your rights as a human to have a driver's license. While other states are still in progress to offer driver's license, there are 12 states that offer driver's licenses to immigrants including Distric of Colombia.

States Offering Driver's License to Immigrants:

  • California
  • Colorado
  • Connecticut
  • Delaware
  • Hawaii
  • Illinois
  • New Mexico
  • Nevada
  • Vermont
  • Washington
  • District of Colombia

California Assembly Bill 60:

Due to this bill, if a California resident is unable to prove their legal presence they are still eligible to apply for a driver's license.

Special License that does not:

  • May NOT be used for identification purposes.
  • MAY be used to drive anywhere throughout the state of California.
  • Has a distinguishing feature and notice on the front of the license indicating that it is for driving purposes only, per the AB-60 law (i.e. “DP" instead of “DL").
  • Cannot be used against the license holder to determine their immigration status or citizenship, or detain them in any way for reasons other than valid traffic violations.
